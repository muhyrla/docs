A **credit** is the unit of the bot's work. Instead of separate limits for answers, uploads, and learning, everything draws from one balance.

## What costs what

| Action | Cost |
|---|---|
| One delivered answer in Discord | 1 credit |
| One learned Q&A pair (auto-learning) | 3 credits |
| Uploading a file | 2 credits |
| Indexing a web page | 2 credits |
| Crawling a documentation site | 15 credits (per site, any number of pages) |

Reading the dashboard, analytics, and the calendar are free — credits are only spent when the bot produces or ingests knowledge.

## Your monthly allowance

At the start of each billing cycle your workspace receives its plan's monthly credits (100 on Free, 500 on Pro, 1,000 on Team). Unused monthly credits **don't roll over** — the allowance resets with the new cycle.

Credits from promo codes or a plan grant come with their own expiry date. When you spend credits, the ones expiring soonest are used first, so nothing goes to waste.

## Running out

When the balance hits zero, the bot stops answering and ingesting until credits arrive — either at the next cycle reset or from a promo code. Your knowledge base, settings, and history are unaffected.

You can see your current balance, where it came from, and when each portion expires on the **Billing** page.
